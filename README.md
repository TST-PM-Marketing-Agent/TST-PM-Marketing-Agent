What do our agents (PM & Marketing) do in the company?
Product Manager Agent
•	Role: Owns product vision and roadmap.
•	Responsibilities: Gather user feedback; prioritize features (MoSCoW method); create roadmaps; validate ideas with prototypes.
•	Inputs: Customer data, sales feedback. Outputs: Product specs, roadmaps, feature backlog.
•	Behaviors: Runs A/B ideation; integrates with Engineering for feasibility checks. Tools: Survey APIs, Jira simulator.
Marketing Agent
•	Role: Generates demand and brand awareness.
•	Responsibilities: Run campaigns; SEO/content; analyze channels; A/B test ads.
•	Inputs: Product details, buyer personas. Outputs: Campaign plans, metrics (CAC, LTV).
•	Behaviors: Creates social posts/emails; tracks ROI. Tools: Google Analytics API, ad platforms.
The Product Manager (PM) Agent acts as the lead organizer for the project, translating the CEO’s big-picture goals into a clear plan of action. Its main job is to keep the company focused by ranking tasks—identifying what is essential versus what can wait—so the team builds exactly what the market needs. By collecting feedback from customers and looking at market trends, this agent creates the "blueprints" or instruction sets that tell the rest of the company what the final product should look like. To keep the workflow moving, the PM Agent regularly checks in with the Engineering Agent to make sure a feature is actually possible to build and then hands off the final details to the Marketing Agent so they can start the promotional rollout.
The Marketing Agent serves as the company’s primary promoter, responsible for turning the PM’s technical plans into messages that attract customers. Instead of focusing on the "how" of building the product, this agent focuses on building awareness through social media posts, email outreach, and digital ads. It uses details about our target audience to create these campaigns and then tracks ROI, to check if ad money is actually bringing in enough new business to be worth the cost. Within the company's shared messaging system, the Marketing Agent passes lists of potential customers to the Sales Agent and automatically triggers a request to the CEO whenever it needs approval to spend more than $10,000 on a new initiative.
